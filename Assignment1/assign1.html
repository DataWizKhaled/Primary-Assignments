MINYA GOVERNORATE

You Should Watch And Enjoy


The city of Minya is indeed one of the beautiful cities in Egypt, often referred to as the "Bride of Upper Egypt." Situated in the northern part of Upper Egypt, specifically on the western bank of the Nile River, Minya boasts important landmarks. This article takes you on a tour of its significant attractions, services, and unique features.

"The Bride of Upper Egypt" has adopted the statue of Nefertiti as its emblem, set amidst a green spot symbolizing its agricultural activity, which dominates most of its areas.

The banks of the Nile in the city of Minya, adorned with the Egyptian flag, are embellished to welcome residents during the governorate's celebrations of its 103rd National Day. The festivities commemorate the locals' resistance against a train carrying British soldiers during the 1919 revolution, resulting in their deaths, including their leader.

The city of Mattay is one of the centers of Minya Governorate. The name "Mattay" dates back to the Pharaonic era, meaning "lowland," and in another account, it is associated with the French campaign, where it was called "Mataa," referring to the campaign's horses.
